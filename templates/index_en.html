InBraille

About project

InBraille is an innovative tool from Pernambuco that converts texts to Braille and exports them in both ASCII format and in STL plates for 3D printing. With this solution, it is possible to increase accessibility and inclusion, allowing anyone to create tactile materials for people with visual impairments.

In addition to being an accessible and easy-to-use technology, this project has a significant social impact, enabling the production of texts, labels, informational signs and educational materials in Braille in an accessible and personalized way. Whether for educational, professional or everyday purposes, this tool aims to make information truly universal. The goal is to democratize access to Braille through maker culture and 3D printing, providing autonomy and independence to those who need it.

See more about this tool on its page at Ciência Embarcada.

The tool

Please note: In Brazilian alphabet, the braille to text conversion has problems with some characters. For example, the letter "&" has the same braille as the letter "ç", for this reason, the conversion will be made to the letter "ç".

Legend of Advanced Options:

  • Unique Plate:

    If checked, the phrase will be in a single plate.

  • Unique Width:

    If checked, the width of all plates will be the same.

  • Rounded:

    If checked, the edges will be rounded. Unavailable if the "Unique Plate" option is checked and the "Unique Width" option is not checked.

  • Plate Thickness:

    The thickness of the plate in millimeters.

  • Text Alignment:

    The alignment of the text. Unavailable if the "Unique Plate" option is not checked or the "Unique Width" option is not checked.

  • Dots Resolution:

    The resolution of the braille dots. By server limitations, the maximum value is 150 and the minimum is 2.
